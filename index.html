Privacy Policy

Your privacy is our priority

Last Updated: December 24, 2025

Complete On-Device Processing

CameraLens processes everything locally on your device. We do not collect, store, or transmit your images, detected text, or any personal data to external servers. Your privacy is protected by design.

Introduction

Welcome to CameraLens, developed by Genstellar. This Privacy Policy explains our commitment to protecting your privacy and details how we handle information when you use our mobile application.

CameraLens is built on a foundation of privacy-first design principles. All image processing, text recognition, and barcode scanning occur entirely on your device using Google ML Kit's on-device models.

Information We Collect

Data We Do NOT Collect

CameraLens does not collect, store, transmit, or process any of the following:

  • Camera images or video footage
  • Detected or recognized text content
  • Scanned barcode or QR code data
  • Identified objects or image labels
  • Personal identifying information
  • Precise location data
  • Usage patterns or analytics
  • Device identifiers or advertising IDs
  • Contacts, messages, or other device data

On-Device Processing Only

All of the following operations are performed exclusively on your device and never leave it:

  • Real-time image processing from your camera feed
  • Optical Character Recognition (OCR) for text detection
  • Object identification through image labeling
  • Barcode and QR code scanning with data interpretation

Permissions Explained

CameraLens requires certain Android permissions to function. Here's exactly why each permission is needed and how it's used:

Camera — Essential for capturing images to perform real-time detection, text recognition, and barcode scanning. Camera access is only active while the app is open and in use.

Internet — Used exclusively to fetch public metadata (page titles, descriptions, preview images) when you scan a QR code containing a website URL. This provides you with a preview before visiting the site.

Location (Fine & Coarse) — Required by Android's security framework to enable WiFi connection features. Used only when you scan a WiFi QR code and choose to connect. Your actual location is never tracked, accessed, or stored.

WiFi State & Change WiFi State — Enables checking WiFi availability and connecting to networks from scanned WiFi QR codes. Only activated when you use WiFi QR code features.

Vibrate — Provides haptic feedback for button presses and interactions, enhancing the user experience with tactile responses.

Flashlight — Allows you to manually enable the camera flash for improved scanning visibility in low-light environments.

Google ML Kit Integration

CameraLens leverages Google ML Kit for powerful on-device machine learning capabilities. ML Kit provides:

  • Image Labeling to identify and classify objects in real-time
  • Text Recognition for accurate optical character recognition
  • Barcode Scanning supporting QR codes and multiple barcode formats

Important Privacy Note: All ML Kit models are downloaded directly to your device during app installation or first use. No image data or detection results are ever transmitted to Google's servers or any external services.

Third-Party Services

URL Metadata Retrieval

When you scan a QR code containing a website URL, CameraLens may fetch public metadata from that website to display a preview. This request goes directly from your device to the website without any intermediary services or tracking.

What We Don't Use

CameraLens intentionally excludes:

  • Analytics platforms (Google Analytics, Firebase Analytics, etc.)
  • Advertising networks or SDKs
  • User behavior tracking services
  • Cloud storage or backup services
  • Third-party crash reporting with personal data
  • Social media integrations or SDKs

Data Storage & Retention

CameraLens operates with a zero-storage privacy model:

  • All processing occurs in volatile memory (RAM) only
  • No images are saved to permanent storage
  • Detection results are cleared when you switch modes or exit the app
  • No persistent logs of your activity are maintained
  • Temporary data in memory is automatically released by the system

Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not share, sell, rent, or disclose any user data to third parties because we fundamentally do not collect any user data. Your camera feed and all detection results remain exclusively on your device under your control.

Children's Privacy

CameraLens does not knowingly collect information from anyone, including children under 13 years of age. Our zero-data-collection approach means the app is inherently safe for users of all ages. However, we recommend parental supervision when children use camera-enabled applications.

Security Measures

Since all processing occurs locally and no data is transmitted to external servers, your information remains protected within your device's security architecture. We recommend:

  • Keeping your device's operating system and security patches up to date
  • Using strong device security (PIN, password, biometric authentication)
  • Exercising caution when scanning QR codes from unknown or untrusted sources
  • Reviewing app permissions regularly in your device settings

Your Privacy Rights

Under various data protection regulations (GDPR, CCPA, etc.), you have certain rights regarding your personal data. However, since CameraLens does not collect, store, or process your personal data:

  • There is no data for us to access, export, or delete
  • You maintain complete control over all processing on your device
  • You can uninstall the app at any time to remove all associated data
  • No data persists on external servers after uninstallation

International Compliance

CameraLens is designed to comply with major data protection regulations worldwide:

  • GDPR (European Union) — No personal data collection means no data subject obligations
  • CCPA (California) — Zero data collection eliminates disclosure requirements
  • PIPEDA (Canada) — Privacy-by-design architecture ensures compliance
  • LGPD (Brazil) — On-device processing aligns with data minimization principles

Since all processing is local, there are no international data transfers, eliminating cross-border data protection concerns.
